Artificial Intelligence

Any technique that lets a machine act in ways that would require intelligence if a human did it — the widest possible frame.

Systems that improve from data instead of being explicitly programmed — the engine inside almost everything we now call "AI."

03 · The architecture you'll understand cold

Attention is the idea
that changed everything.

One mechanism — every token looking at every other token — now powers translation, code, vision, and reasoning. You will derive it, not just cite it.

The
model
attends
to
itself
Attention(Q,K,V) = softmax(QKᵗ / √dₖ) · V

Self-attention lets every position in a sequence weigh every other position directly — no recurrence, no information bottleneck, just learned relevance.

Multi-head attention runs that process several times in parallel, each head free to specialize in a different kind of relationship.

Positional encoding reintroduces order, since attention alone has none — and feed-forward layers hold most of what the model actually "knows."

You'll build this from first principles: the math, the intuition, and the reason it parallelizes across GPUs in a way recurrent networks never could.
